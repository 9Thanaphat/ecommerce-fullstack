# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech hobbyists and enthusiasts with precise taste: keyboard collectors, camera nerds, desk-setup obsessives, and people who care about the object itself — its weight, finish, and function. They shop with patience, not impulse. They read specs. They compare switches. They notice when a product page is sloppy. Context: they're at their desk, probably late, researching their next deliberate purchase.

## Product Purpose

A curated dark-aesthetic e-commerce store selling items that share a sensibility more than a category: mechanical keyboards, vintage lenses, luxury fragrances, leather goods, precision accessories, and objects that reward attention. The store is the edit. Success means a customer who lands on the site and immediately knows this is not Amazon — they feel like they found something.

## Brand Personality

Dark, minimal, precise.

Voice: terse, confident, non-promotional. The product speaks; the copy gets out of the way. No hype, no urgency tricks, no superlatives. Just what the thing is and why it matters.

## Anti-references

- Amazon, Lazada, Shopee — loud, cluttered, deal-focused. Everything this isn't.
- Generic SaaS landing pages — cream backgrounds, gradient text, hero metrics.
- Techwear fast-fashion aesthetic — dark but derivative and over-styled.
- Aliexpress product photography — white-background grids, no editorial sense.

## Design Principles

1. **The object is the hero.** Product images, materials, and form carry the brand. The UI recedes. Never let chrome compete with the product.
2. **Precision signals quality.** Tight spacing, exact alignment, deliberate type scale. Sloppiness is a trust failure. Every pixel is a signal.
3. **Restraint is a choice, not a default.** Every element that remains has earned its place. If it doesn't serve the product or the customer's decision, remove it.
4. **Dark is not decoration.** The dark surface is load-bearing — it's the neutral void that makes products read as lit objects. Never warm it with cream or tint it with purple; keep it void.
5. **Copy earns its place.** One precise sentence beats a paragraph of adjectives. Specific details (weight, switch actuation, leather origin) over marketing copy.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum; target AAA for body text. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable. Reduced-motion alternative for all animations. No color-only information encoding. Product images must have descriptive alt text.
